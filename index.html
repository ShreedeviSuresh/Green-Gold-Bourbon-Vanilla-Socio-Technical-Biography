Conclusion
"The product's marketable identity depends, quite fundamentally, on how much of its actual history remains hidden from the person buying it."

Tracing this vanilla pod from flower to bin makes visible a set of relationships its packaging works hard to conceal. The pollination paradox ties European consumption directly to precarious manual labour in Madagascar. Processing value is captured by intermediaries rather than producers. Organic certification covers farming but not high-emission logistics. The brand name carries colonial history that has been aestheticised out of recognition. And the end-of-life stage reproduces linear waste logic within a product category marketed as sustainable.

Reporting from National Geographic and The Guardian (2025) adds organised theft and violence to the picture â€” consequences of a price structure the market created but does not distribute justly. Reforming this means changing who captures value and who absorbs risk â€” questions that go well beyond consumer choice and into the structural politics of global trade.
